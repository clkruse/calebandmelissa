THE INVITATION

We were excited to create a wedding invite that could be a small piece of art capturing where we're getting married. Here's how it was made.

We worked with a local letterpress printer and artist, Li Jiang at Lemoncheese Press, to design and print our invitations and see the steps along the way. It started with a sketch. We shared some ideas of what we had in mind, and Li drew a picture of what the print might look like. After a few conversations, we arrived at this concept.

To go from sketch to letterpress print, Li created a plate for each color in the piece. Once inked, the plates work a bit like stamps, leaving a mark wherever there’s a raised surface. But the plates aren’t carved directly. Instead, Li meticulously etched away an opaque sheet in any location where the color should be present. These sheets were then laid over a photosensitive polymer and exposed to UV light. Any etched location in the sheet allows light to shine onto the polymer and causes it to harden. The unexposed places are then rinsed away, leaving a raised surface for printing.

A single plate is loaded into the letterpress, and all copies are printed with that color. Layer by layer, the colors are combined in order to create the piece on your invitation.

We hope you enjoy the print as much as we do!
